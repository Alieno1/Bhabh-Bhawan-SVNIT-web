Discover Bhabha Bhavan: Home to SVNIT's Future Leaders

Welcome to Bhabha Bhavan, SVNIT's dedicated hostel for male students, named in honor of Dr. Homi J. Bhabha, a pioneer of Indian nuclear science. Our mission is to provide a safe and supportive environment that enhances the academic and personal growth of our residents.

Bhabha Bhavan, inaugurated in 2012, comprises A-wing and B-wing, spanning from the 1st floor to the 8th floor, totalling 480 rooms. Among its facilities, there is a T.V. & T.T. Hall on the 5th floor, a C.C.F. Hall on the 4th floor, a reading area on the 2nd floor, and a conference room on the 3rd floor. Additionally, there is a night canteen located on the ground floor of the A-wing parking area. The guest rooms are situated on the 2nd and 3rd floors, with a total of 4 guest rooms available. The parking areas for both wings, a volleyball ground for the B-wing, and various gardens both inside and outside the wings are also part of the amenities provided by Bhabha Bhavan.

Homi Jehangir Bhabha (1909 - 1966)

Homi Jehangir Bhabha was a brilliant Indian physicist, often referred to as the "father of the Indian nuclear program". His scientific pursuits not only propelled India into the realm of nuclear science but also left an indelible mark on the global scientific community. Under Bhabha's leadership, India's nuclear ambitions took concrete shape.

One of Bhabha's seminal contributions came in the form of the 'Bhabha Scattering', a theory that elucidated the behavior of cosmic rays in the Earth's atmosphere. This ground-breaking work not only earned him accolades within the scientific community but also propelled him onto the global stage.

Bhabha's crowning achievement came with the establishment of the Atomic Energy Commission of India in 1948. Recognizing the transformative potential of nuclear energy, he championed the cause of harnessing atomic power for peaceful purposes, laying the groundwork for India's nuclear program.

Thanganat: SVNIT's Ultimate Multi-Night Garba Fiesta!

"Thanganat," the annual multi-night Garba event organized by the inmates of Bhabha Bhavan at SVNIT, is a transformative experience that transcends the ordinary, immersing attendees in the rich tapestry of Gujarati folk dance and culture. With a gathering of over 3500 participants, ranging from students to esteemed faculty, the event pulsates with the vibrant spirit of the SVNIT community. The evening commences with a soul-stirring aarti dedicated to Goddess Durga, infusing the air with reverence and joy. As anticipation mounts, the aroma of Mahaprasad fills the atmosphere, eagerly awaited and relished by all. Under the twinkling night sky, the Garba dance takes center stage, setting the night ablaze with its infectious rhythms and boundless energy.

What distinguishes "Thanganat" is its inclusivity, welcoming students from all backgrounds to partake in the joy of Garba. From the graceful leaps of seasoned dancers to the tentative steps of the shy, each individual adds their own unique charm to the dance floor, creating an enchanting mosaic of movement and melody. With every beat of the dhol and every swirl of the dandiya, "Thanganat" crafts an enchanting experience that leaves a lasting impression, ensuring that the memories of this cultural spectacle linger long after the music fades into the night.
